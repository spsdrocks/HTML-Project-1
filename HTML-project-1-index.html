Upgrades



Conversions

Engine Swap

  • Keep stock unless you need the power.

Drivetrain Swap

  • Keep stock unless you really want to change.

Aspiration

  • Keep stock unless you need the power.

Body Kit

  • Can increase handling and looks cool.
  • Cannot use adjustable aero if you apply

Aero and Apperance

Front Bumper

  • Useful for adding front grip and custom aero.
  • Better to build without, optional.
  • If non adjustable, good for tweaking PI later

Rear Wing

  • If it increases PI, do not use unless you have the extra budget or need more rear grip.
  • If it decreases PI than use it if needed.
  • Good to have custom aero tuning
  • Use to tweak PI if not adjustable

Rear Bumper

  • Unsure

Side Skirts

  • Unsure

Hood

  • Unsure

Tires and Rims

Tire Compound

  • For AWD, stock or 1-2 levels up.
  • For RWD and FWD, semi-slick is best if you have the PI budget.

Front Tire Width

  • For FWD upgrade for less understeer.
  • For AWD and RWD upgrade only if needed or have the PI budget.

Rear Tire Width

  • AWD and FWD use it if you need a little extra rear grip.
  • RWD always use for extra rear grip unless you don't have the PI budget.

Rim Style

  • Use this to dial in your PI at the end.

Front and Rear Rim Size

  • Use bigger rims to make your car more responsive.

Front and Rear Track Width

  • AWD and FWD, use to increase overall handling of the car.
  • RWD upgrading rear while leaving front stock helps for handling.
  • Use to dial oversteer and understeer.
  • More rear spacing is more stability and understeer.
  • More front spacing is more responsive and oversteer.

Drivetrain

Clutch

  • A more manual control scheme makes this less useful.
  • On automatic and manual clutchless this helps decrease shift time.

Transmission

  • Sport transmission is most versatile upgrade.
  • Use race if you have spare PI or want more gears.
  • Unlocks final drive tuning.

Driveline

  • Good for PI tweaks at the end.
  • Will reduce rate and increase rev speed

Differential

  • Allways upgrade to unlock differential tuning at a low PI cost.
  • Use race differential for full tuning.

Platform and Handling

Brakes

  • Race brakes will almost always be useful.

Springs and Dampers

  • Race springs and dampers are best.
  • Unlocks full spring, damper, and alignment tuning.

Front and Rear Anti Roll Bars

  • Allways get to unlock tuning at a cheap PI cost.

Chassis Reinforcement and Roll Cage

  • For each class above stock, add one level of reinforcement.
  • Don't use full roll cage unless you really want to.

Weight Reduction

  • Here you chose to do full weight reduction or full power build.
  • For online racing, power is normally better but both are good.
  • Weight reduction will increase handling.
  • If you do not care about PI, or have extra, max out weight and power.

Intake

Exhaust

  • Highly valuable to make our car better.

Oil and Cooling

  • Also good at a cheap PI cost.

Intercooler

  • Good if your car has a turbo or supercharger.

Camshaft

  • Useful but very expensive so only worth it if you have the extra budget.

Fuel System

  • Good for power if you have the budget.

Ignition

  • Good for power if you have the budget.

Rotors and Compression

  • Good for power if you have the budget.

Flywheel

  • Good to slightly increase PI.
  • Makes revs more responsive.

Supercharger

  • Upgrade if your car has a supercharger to add power.

Turbo

  • Save for last if you have the PI budget.

Displacement

  • Save for last if you have the extra PI budget.



Tuning



Differential

Front

  • Acceleration
    • Set to 10-50%, lower for more oversteer and higher for more understeer.
    • Lower is better for race.
  • Deceleration
    • Keep lower than your acceleration setting.
    • Start at 0% go up from there.

Rear

  • Acceleration
    • Set to 50-90%, lower for less powered older cars, higher for more powerful cars.
  • Deceleration
    • Keep lower than your acceleration setting.
    • Start at 0% go up from there.

Center

  • Keep from 50-80%.
  • Higher is better for track builds, start at 70%.

Brakes

Balance

  • Keep stock at start and adjust after test.
  • More front makes more oversteer, more rear is more stability.

Pressure

  • Adjust your total braking force.
  • Keep stock most of the time.

Aero

Front

  • Adjusts the amount of downforce applied to the front of the car.
  • More downforce for grip at speeds and much better cornering.
  • Makes car feel more responsive and snappy.
  • Makes car feel more responsive and snappy.
  • Keep stock until test.

Rear

  • Adjusts the amount of downforce on the rear end of the car.
  • More downforce for grip at speeds and much better cornering.
  • Less downforce for top speed and acceleration.
  • Keep stock until test.

Damping


Rebound Stiffness

  • Keep stock unless you must change it.
  • Increase stiffness for the end that is not losing grip.
  • Decrease stiffness for the end that is losing grip.

Bump Stiffness

  • Generally have 50-75% of your rebound stiffness.
  • Lower by a few notches at start and adjust later if needed.

Springs

Springs

  • Less front stiffness increases oversteer and less rear stiffness is understeer.
  • Overall higher makes less grip but more responsive.
  • Overall lower makes more grip but more understeer.
  • Lower front for AWD and FWD keep same and adjust as needed for RWD.

Ride Hight

  • Overall make a few notches lower.
  • Lowers center of gravity.
  • Low as possible without bottoming out

Anti Roll Bars

Anti Roll Bars

  • Low front causes oversteer.
  • Low rear causes understeer.
  • For FWD and AWD 50% of stock front setting and 150% of stock rear setting.
  • Not sure for RWD.

Alignment

Camber

  • Always keep camber negative.
  • Bump it up one or two notches up from stock in both settings.

Toe

  • Toe out is oversteer and response.
  • Toe in is understeer and stability.
  • For FWD and AWD go 1-3 notches toward toe out for oversteer.
  • For RWD put rear toe in 1-2 notches for good rear stability in corners.

Front Caster

  • Higher increases camber in corners.
  • Stay in between 5 and 7 degrees for most cars.

Gearing

Forward Gears

  • Increase or decrease final drive to make it have the most speed.
  • The end of the last gear at the end of the chart or a little past.
  • For high power RWD put 1st and 2nd towards speed a little bit to control throttle.
  • For AWD and FWD keep gears stock.

Tires

Tire Pressure

  • Higher is more sudden loss of grip and more responsive.
  • Lower is more controllable loss of grip and more stability.
  • Keep from 1.8 to 2.3.
